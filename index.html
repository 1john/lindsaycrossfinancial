Our Core Offerings

Medicare

Our team can help you navigate the complexities of Part A & B, find policies to cover gaps (Advantage plans or Supplements), and stay updated on the industry. We offer a variety of plans to fit your needs and budget to make sure that you are protected.

Prescription Drug Coverage

Prescription Drugs can be some of the more tedious and frustrating plans to navigate. We help you choose the right combination of plan and pharmacies to make sure you never overpay for your prescriptions.

Dental, Hearing, and Vision

Ideal for filling healthcare gaps – these services aren't typically included in standard medical insurance. Many plans offer no waiting periods on preventative care, set annual benefit limits, and allow you to choose in- or out-of-network providers. They're flexible, affordable, and designed to keep your mouth, eyes, and ears in top condition.

Health Insurance

Whether you are moving on from group health coverage, looking for coverage for a spouse after moving to Medicare, or just shopping plans, we can help you see what this coverage looks like in your area and get you set with a plan that works for you.

Life Insurance

Secure your family's financial future with life insurance. We offer term life, whole life, and universal life policies. Our team can help you determine the best coverage amount and type for your situation.

401K Rollovers and Retirement Planning

401K rollovers move funds from your old employer plan into a qualified retirement account without triggering taxes or penalties. Plan your retirement with Lindsay Cross Financial. We offer a range of retirement planning options to help you achieve your long-term financial goals.

Creditable coverage is prescription drug coverage (for example, from an employer, union, TRICARE, Indian Health Service, or the Department of Veterans Affairs) that is expected to pay, on average, at least as much as Medicare's standard prescription drug coverage. Consumers with creditable coverage when they become eligible for Medicare can generally keep that coverage without paying a penalty if they decide to enroll in Medicare prescription drug coverage later.

The organization providing the prescription drug coverage must inform the consumer annually (generally in a letter or organization newsletter) if the prescription drug coverage is creditable coverage. The consumer will need this information if they later enroll in a Medicare Plan that provides Part D prescription drug coverage.

If a consumer does not enroll in Part D coverage when they are first eligible, or if they have a break in Part D coverage of 63 consecutive days or longer, they may be subject to a Part D late enrollment penalty (LEP). The Part D LEP is required by law and will be calculated by CMS and added to the consumer's monthly Part D premium for as long as they are enrolled Part D coverage, even if the consumer enrolls in a $0 premium plan. To help ensure a positive consumer experience, it is important that consumers understand what the LEP is and that it will apply regardless of which carrier or plan the consumer chooses.

Note: The LEP may be reduced or eliminated if the consumer qualifies for the Low-Income Subsidy Program (i.e., Extra Help).
